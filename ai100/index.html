AI100  —  Foundations of Artificial Intelligence
Professor Chris Rosa
AI100  ·  Sacred Heart University
Course Description  ·  Undergraduate Catalog

This course provides an introduction to the history, terminology, and foundational concepts of artificial intelligence (AI). Students will explore contemporary AI technologies and their applications across various industries, considering both the opportunities and risks associated with AI's growing influence. Special attention is given to ethical tensions, data privacy, and broader societal challenges. Students gain introductory exposure to machine learning concepts and engage in hands-on learning in the AI Lab, building a strong base for deeper exploration in later courses. Designed for non-technical majors, the course aims to spark curiosity and strengthen critical thinking about AI's implications within each student's academic discipline. No prior computer science experience is required.

3 Credits
Prereq: None

Course Topics
Foundations of AI

Trace the history of AI, learn core terminology, and build a shared language for understanding what AI is, what it is not, and how foundational concepts evolved into modern systems.

AI in Society

Examine contemporary AI applications across industries while critically evaluating benefits, risks, ethical tensions, privacy concerns, and the societal impact of increasingly automated decision-making.

Machine Learning and AI Lab

Get an accessible introduction to machine learning and practice experiential, hands-on activities in the AI Lab that connect technical ideas to your own discipline, with no prior CS background required.
